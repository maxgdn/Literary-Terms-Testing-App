personification = A figure of speech where human qualities are given to animals, objects or ideas
protagonist = The leading character, hero, or heroine of a drama or other literary work
sarcasm = The use of irony to mock or convey contempt
satire = A literary composition, in verse or prose, in which irony, human folly and vice are held up to scorn, derision, or ridicule
sestet = A stanza or a poem of six lines; specifically :  the last six lines of an Italian sonnet
soliloquy = A device often used in drama when a character speaks to himself or herself, relating thoughts and feelings, thereby also sharing them with the audience
stream of consciousness = a narrative mode or device that depicts the multitudinous thoughts and feelings which pass through the mind
tragedy =  a form of drama based on human suffering or misfortune
symbol = Something used for or regarded as representing something else; a material object representing something deeper, often something immaterial
in media res = a Latin phrase used by the poet Horace; it means in the middle of things.
